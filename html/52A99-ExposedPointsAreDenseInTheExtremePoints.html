exposed points are dense in the extreme points


Definition.

Let K⊂ℝn be a closed convex set. A point p∈K is called an exposed point if there is an n-1 dimensional hyperplane whose intersection with K is p alone.

Theorem (Strasziewicz).

Let K⊂Rn be a closed convex set. Then the set of exposed points is dense in the set of extreme points.

For example, let C⁢(p) denote the closed ball in ℝ2 of radius 1 and centered at p. Then take K to be the convex hull of C⁢(-1,0) and C⁢(1,0). The points (-1,1), (-1,-1), (1,1), and (1,-1) are extreme points, but they are not exposed points.
