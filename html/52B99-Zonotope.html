zonotope


A zonotope is a polytope which can be obtained as the Minkowski sum (http://planetmath.org/MinkowskiSum3) of finitely many closed line segments in ℝn. Three-dimensional zonotopes are also sometimes called zonohedra. Zonotopes are dual to finite hyperplane arrangements. They are centrally symmetric, compact, convex sets.

For example, the unit n-cube is the Minkowski sum of the line segments from the origin to the standard unit vectors ei for 1≤i≤n. A hexagon is also a zonotope; for example, the Minkowski sum of the line segments based at the origin with endpoints at (1,0), (1,1), and (0,1) is a hexagon. In fact, any projection of an n-cube is a zonotope.

The prism of a zonotope is always a zonotope, but the pyramid of a zonotope need not be. In particular, the n-simplex (http://planetmath.org/HomologyTopologicalSpace) is only a zonotope for n≤1.
